frontend/src/app/review-queue/page.tsx

Issue: The inline-block min-w-max wrapper from the 
previous fix prevents the panel from expanding to 
fill available space when the sidebar collapses.

Root cause: inline-block min-w-max shrinks to content 
width and never grows, so it ignores available space.

Fix: Remove the inline-block min-w-max wrapper entirely.
Use min-width on the header and table instead — 
they scroll when viewport is too narrow but expand 
to fill all available space when there is room.

BEFORE:
<div className="bg-white border border-slate-200 rounded h-full">
  <div className="overflow-x-auto">
    <div className="inline-block min-w-max align-top">
      <div className="px-3 py-2 bg-[#1F3864] ... min-w-full">
        Draft Completed
      </div>
      <DataTable ... className="w-full" />
      <div className="... min-w-full">Tip: ...</div>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

AFTER:
<div className="bg-white border border-slate-200 rounded h-full">
  <div className="overflow-x-auto w-full">
    <div className="px-3 py-2 bg-[#1F3864] text-white 
      text-xs font-semibold border-b border-slate-200 
      min-w-[700px] w-full">
      Draft Completed
    </div>
    <DataTable ... className="w-full min-w-[700px]" />
    <div className="text-xs text-slate-500 px-3 py-2 
      border-t border-slate-200 min-w-[700px] w-full">
      Tip: Double-click a row or press Enter/Space to open.
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

Why this works:
- w-full makes the container fill all available space 
  when the sidebar expands or collapses
- min-w-[700px] ensures table never collapses below 
  a usable minimum width
- When viewport < 700px: overflow-x-auto scrolls
- When viewport > 700px: everything expands to fill space
- Header and table always have the same min-width so 
  they always scroll together perfectly
