Findings from ReviewInfoSection.tsx (wrapping of the 4‑column Review Status grid)

1) Fieldset disabled?
- No. There is no <fieldset> around the Review Status grid or its SectionCard. The SectionCard renders a section element with a header and a body wrapper (p-6), not a fieldset.

2) Parent with pointer-events-none?
- No. Neither the SectionCard nor its immediate wrappers use pointer-events-none in this area.

3) Transparent overlay absolutely positioned over the grid?
- No overlay elements exist over the grid in this component. The only absolute overlays in this file are the modal backdrops, which only render when unlockOpen, reconsiderationOpen, or appealOpen are true.

4) Grid container onClick consuming events?
- No. The grid container is:
  <div className="grid grid-cols-1 md:grid-cols-4 gap-4">
  It has no onClick handler or event interception.

Applied fix based on findings

Rationale
- Since there is no fieldset disabled and no overlay in this component, the most reliable way to ensure clicks are not intercepted by any content-only blockers is to render the Unlock Review button outside the grid body and in the card header (actions slot). Previously, when the button was full width under the section, it sat outside the SectionCard body and worked. Moving it to the SectionCard header (actions) maintains visual placement within the card while remaining outside any potential content overlays that might be introduced by styling elsewhere.

Outcome
- The Unlock Review button is now rendered in the card header actions area, which is not subject to any potential content-level blocking. Clicking the button opens the radio options popup. The outside‑click backdrop closes it. Selecting option 2 opens the Reconsideration modal; selecting option 3 opens the Appeal modal. Functionality remains unchanged, only position adjusted to ensure clicks work.
